01
War Kite
Digital Composite

"War Kite"

Inspired by Radiohead's "You and Whose Army?" and personal experience in Pakistan. Explores how the constant presence of war reshapes the adolescent mind. A child holds a fighter jet like a kite — the loss of innocence made tangible. Rugged, grounded texture to achieve a "disturbed" look that captures the subconscious weight of conflict.

02
Nature's Icarus
Digital Photography

"Nature's Icarus"

A study of how life subtly imitates art. Shot at early morning to catch the water's glare at the perfect angle — the light turns a bird into a silhouette falling into brightness. A natural reimagining of the Icarus myth, documenting a brief moment where the environment mimics classical tragedy.

2024 Photography
03
Frutiger Fever
Digital Composite — Layered Negatives

"Frutiger Fever"

An experiment with the Frutiger Aero core — the aesthetic of early digital optimism. A negative was created to distance the image from realism, then overlaid with soft light, transparency, and smooth architectural forms. A place that exists between nostalgia and the future. What started as a visual study became a reflection on how design once imagined progress as calm, luminous, and reassuring.

2024 Composite
04
"Photography"
Digital Collage — Documentary + Editorial

"Photography"

A critique of how photography, especially in Pakistan, is often reduced to images of the poor and vulnerable circulated for attention or validation. A raw street photograph layered with a magazine cover reading "Photography for a Profitable Career." The image asks: where does documentation end and exploitation begin?

2024 Collage
05
Thought Process
Digital Degradation — Visual Storytelling

"Thought Process"

Simulating the "fading effect" of long-term memory using stochastic dithering on a high-contrast subject. The environment is stripped away, leaving the playground suspended in a black abyss — highlighting the selective nature of memory. The aesthetic of "found data": as if pulled from a corrupted file deep within a digital archive.

06
2Falcons
Photography — Composite

"2Falcons"

Exploring the intersection of biological evolution and mechanical engineering. The silhouettes of a peregrine falcon and an F-16 Fighting Falcon, layered. Human innovation as an echo of nature's perfected designs. High-grain monochrome strips away the distinction between feathers and titanium — two predators, two eras, one sky.

About

Bilal Hussain is a visual artist and photographer whose practice centres on digital composite work, documentary photography, and image manipulation.

Working at the intersection of memory, conflict, and the subconscious, his images probe the psychological terrain between personal experience and wider cultural forces — war, technology, exploitation, and the act of looking itself.

Rooted in Pakistan, shaped by the aesthetics of early digital culture and contemporary critical theory.
