Act I — The Dream / The Beach
SHOT 01
Wide — Beach & Classroom Merged
Wide shot reveals a surreal scene: school desks and chairs arranged naturally on a beach. Warm, overexposed light. Ocean in background. Students scattered. One student (our lead) is slumped asleep at a front desk. Reference: the bed-on-beach scene from Eternal Sunshine — grounded, not fantastical.
Wide / Drone or Dolly Ambient ocean waves, faint school ambience layered
SHOT 02
Close-up — Asleep at Desk
Tight shot of the lead's face resting on his folded arms at the desk. Peaceful. Sound of the waves. Eyes closed. Slight breeze moves his hair. Hold for a beat.
Close-up, 50mm or 85mm Waves dominant now
SHOT 03
Lead Wakes, Stands
Lead stirs, lifts his head, disoriented. He looks around — registers the beach, the desks, the ocean. He stands slowly. In the background, a group of friends are clustered around the last bench, talking. He looks at them.
Medium Wide Keep it dreamlike — no rush, no reaction
SHOT 04
"Where is Yasser?"
Lead turns to his friends at the back bench. He asks. All three point simultaneously toward the shore. Camera follows their fingers to reveal — far away, at the water's edge — Yasser, crouched, washing his shoes in the sea.
LEAD"Where is Yasser?"
Over-shoulder → Pan to Shore
SHOT 05
Yasser at the Shore
Wide shot of Yasser alone at the water's edge, back to camera, crouched, methodically washing his shoes in the shallow waves. Peaceful. The camera slowly pushes in as lead walks toward him from across the sand.
Slow push-in, wide → medium Waves louder. No music yet.
SHOT 06
The Monologue — Yasser Speaks
Lead reaches Yasser. He sits or crouches beside him. Beat. He asks. Yasser doesn't look up — still washing, still watching the water. Then, quietly, he speaks. Cut between close-up on Yasser's face and the ocean, or hold on a two-shot.
LEAD"Why are you here alone?"
YASSER"Do you ever think... this moment in time is never coming back? All our 12 years in school — just gone, like that. The way time passes, linearly, to never come back. Wake up."
Two-shot / CU Yasser / Insert: waves Music begins softly here — no lyrics Hold on the silence after "wake up"
Act II — The Snap Back / Reality
SHOT 07
Smash Cut — Real Classroom
Hard cut. A real, ordinary classroom. Fluorescent light, noise, life. Yasser leans over from his desk and taps the lead on the shoulder — or calls his name. "Hey, do you have a pen?" Lead blinks. Returns. Looks around. It's all real.
YASSER (real)"Yo, do you have a pen?"
Medium — handheld, naturalistic Room noise, chairs, voices — full ambience hits No explanation of the dream. Just: life continues.
Act III — The Child / Memory Sequence
SHOT 08
Lead Gets Up — Follows the Child
Lead hands Yasser the pen in a daze. He gets up from his seat. Through the classroom window or door, he spots a small child — a younger version of himself — walking alone down the hallway, or playing in the distance. He follows instinctively.
Tracking shot from behind — lead follows child Music swells softly — no words Child should never look back
SHOT 09
Child on the Playground
The child is playing alone in the school playground — swinging, or running, or just standing and looking around with the wonder of someone who doesn't know how fast it will all go. Lead watches from a distance, unseen. He can't look away.
Wide — lead in foreground, child deep in frame Ambient playground sounds under the music No dialogue. Hold long enough to feel it.
SHOT 10
Close-up — Lead's Face
Tight on his face. He's not sad, not happy — just present. Awake. Like Yasser's words finally landed. The child runs off screen. The lead is left alone in the frame. A beat. Then — cut to the ceremony, or the credits roll.
Extreme close-up — eyes, expression Music peaks quietly, then fades Final image — let it breathe before cut
